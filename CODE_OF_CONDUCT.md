# Code of Conduct

## TL;DR

Be respectful. Help others learn. Don't be a jerk.

## The shorter form

Specter is built and maintained by one person, with contributions from people who care about content workflows. We want it to stay a project that's pleasant to work on and pleasant to receive feedback in. To that end:

- **Be welcoming.** Specter is a project people often discover when they're new to building on top of CMS APIs. Patient, concrete answers help everyone; condescension is a way to lose contributors before they ever submit a PR.
- **Be precise.** "This doesn't work" is less useful than "I expected X, I saw Y, here's how to reproduce." Apply the same precision when reviewing others' work.
- **Be respectful of time.** I (Axel) maintain this in evenings + weekends around running revenue-generating Ghost sites. Don't expect instant turnaround on issues or PRs. I'll get there.
- **Disagree without making it personal.** Technical decisions have tradeoffs. If we disagree on a design call, we're disagreeing about the tradeoffs, not about each other's intelligence or character.
- **No harassment, doxxing, or discrimination.** Goes without saying. It's enforced.

## What happens if there's a problem

If someone's behavior in an issue, PR, or other Specter-related forum makes you uncomfortable, email **`hello@spectersync.com`** with the details. I'll read it, talk to the parties involved if appropriate, and take action ranging from "a private word" to "ban from the project" depending on severity.

I won't share who reported what without your consent.

## The longer form

Specter follows the spirit of the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct.html). If a specific situation isn't covered above, fall back on that document.

## Scope

This applies to all Specter-related spaces: this repo's issues, PRs, discussions, and any external forum (Twitter / X, Mastodon, BlueSky, HN comments, Reddit threads, Discord — wherever Specter is being discussed) when you're representing the project or interacting with other contributors.

---

**Goal**: Specter is a small, focused project. We can keep it that way and stay civil. Thanks for helping.
